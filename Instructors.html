Jounier Taylor-Chief Instructor

Jounier has a passion for learning and teaching. He has been teaching martial arts for over 25 years. Participating and teaching Karate has taken him on many tours around the world, visited many countries and made many friends nation and world wide. He has proudly taught students ranging from beginers to world class level, developing their disciplined training programmes.

Jounier is a qualified teacher gaining a degree in Sociology and Education and a PGCE/Cert Ed. He has numerous fitness qualifications including YMCA dance, Cardio Kick fit, Boxercise (advanced) and BWLA (British Weight Lifters Assosiation). Also, Child protection and First Aid Certificates. He is an international referee and is registered with REP (Sports Council for Wales) WTKA, WUKO, WTKS, EWSKA and Sport England.

During this time he has developed and delivered programmes for youth work. He has worked with various YMCAs with the socially inactive and supporting needs within the homeless. He also teaches in schools and youth clubs, dealing with social issues such as behaviour, the building of trust and presenting a positive role model through classroom lessons, advice, discussion and activity groups. He is also an anti-bullying member of IpowerI Actionworks.

Jounier sees sports and martial arts as a means to communicate and help individuals to reach their full potential in whatever they choose, by instilling discipline, trust, respect, dedication and a willingness to continually learn. His passion, enjoyment and endless enthusiasmleaves a lasting impression with all he comes into contact with.

Chris Bladon-Instructor

Chris spent his childhood in staffordshire and now lives their with his family where he teaches two clubs in Chasetown, as well as running his own business. His martial arts career started at age 5 and he entered his first tournament at aged 8. He won silver in the juniour division of the National Championships and gained his 1st dan black belt aged 11. he was then selected for the England squad and won his first juniour international tournament and gained his 2nd dan.

In 1998 he won the World Championships in Italy and held the title for six years, and the European Championships of which he held the title for four years. Chris then decided his new challenge was to learn the art of teaching Karate and pass on his enjoyment of martial arts.

Since bgining to teach Chris has broadened his skills from Karate to boxing and Mixed Martial Arts, of which he has won 10 amateur boxing fights in the welterweight category and won his first MMA fight in 2008 in just under 2 minutes with a technical knock-out.

Chris has an infectious love for Karate that is enjoyed by his students and by all he meets
